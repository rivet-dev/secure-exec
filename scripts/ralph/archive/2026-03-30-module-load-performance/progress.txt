## Codebase Patterns
- Active module-load benchmark artifacts live in `packages/secure-exec/benchmarks/results/module-load/`; use `summary.md`, `summary.json`, and `analysis.md` as the baseline source of truth before changing perf-sensitive code.
- Every performance story must rerun `pnpm --filter secure-exec bench:module-load` before completion. If a full run is blocked, record the targeted scenario command, blocker, and follow-up story in this file and in `scripts/ralph/prd.json`.
- Each perf progress entry must record before/after warm wall time for the affected scenarios, bridge calls per iteration, and the dominant IPC counters touched by the story (`_loadPolyfill`, `_resolveModule`, `_fsExists`, `Execute` bytes, `BridgeResponse` bytes, or new UDS/session metrics).
- Hono is the low-RPC control; Pi SDK and Pi CLI are the high-RPC workloads. Do not claim success from a single aggregate number without scenario-by-scenario deltas.
- `_loadPolyfill` is the dominant hotspot today, so any bridge/bootstrap/snapshot change must compare `_loadPolyfill` call count, host bridge time, and response bytes against the baseline artifacts.
- The current benchmark excludes `createNodeV8Runtime()` startup from wall time; only the dedicated cold-start control story should change how that number is interpreted.
- New optimization ideas discovered during a story belong in both `docs-internal/todo.md` and a new `passes: false` follow-up entry in `scripts/ralph/prd.json` before the story is marked complete.
